Microsoft Teams
Meetings · Channels · Chat · Files · Collaboration
Getting started4 skills
1
Starter
Setting your status
Keep colleagues informed without interrupting your workflow — add a message that resets automatically.
  • Click your profile picture (top right)
  • Choose: Available, Busy, Do Not Disturb, Away
  • Add a status message: "In lessons until 3:30pm"
  • Set the message to clear automatically after a chosen time
2
Starter
Scheduling a meeting
Create a Teams meeting from the Calendar — a joining link is added automatically to every attendee's invite.
  • Click Calendar in the left sidebar
  • Click New meeting (top right)
  • Add a title, attendees, date and time
  • Save — attendees get a calendar invite with the Teams link
3
Starter
Using channels
Channels organise conversations by topic — important messages never get buried in a single general chat.
  • Use General only for whole-team announcements
  • Create channels for projects, year groups, or subjects
  • Pin important posts with the "…" menu → Pin
  • Use @channel to notify all subscribers to that channel
4
Starter
Sharing files
Files shared in Teams live in SharePoint — accessible to the whole team, version-controlled, and simultaneously editable.
  • Go to the Files tab inside any channel
  • Click Upload to add files from your device
  • Or drag files directly into a chat window
  • Multiple people can edit the same file at the same time
Meetings4 skills
5
Core
Meeting chat
The chat persists after the meeting ends — useful for sharing links, notes, and agreed actions.
  • Open the Chat panel during a meeting (speech bubble icon)
  • Paste links, decisions, or key points as they arise
  • Find the chat after the meeting in the Chat section of Teams
6
Core
Recording meetings
Recordings save to OneDrive automatically and can be shared — essential for absent attendees and minutes.
  • During a meeting: three dots → Start recording
  • All attendees are notified when recording starts
  • Stop recording from the same menu or end the meeting
  • Recording appears in the meeting chat once processed
7
Core
Breakout rooms
Split attendees into smaller groups mid-meeting — organiser-only feature, ideal for discussion or workshop tasks.
  • Click Breakout rooms icon in the meeting toolbar (organiser only)
  • Choose number of rooms; assign attendees manually or automatically
  • Open all rooms at once or individually
  • Send announcements to all rooms from the main meeting
8
Core
Live captions & transcription
Real-time captions improve accessibility; a full transcript can be downloaded as a Word document after the meeting.
  • Three dots → Turn on live captions (captions only, not saved)
  • For a saved transcript: More actions → Start transcription
  • Download: meeting details → Transcript tab
  • Copilot meeting summaries require transcription to be running
Power features3 skills
9
Power
@mentions precisely
Use the right @mention to avoid notification fatigue — not every message needs to reach everyone.
  • @name — notifies one specific person
  • @channel — notifies all channel subscribers
  • @team — notifies all team members (use sparingly)
  • Filter your Activity feed to show only @mentions and replies
10
Power
Pinning tabs to channels
Pin apps and shared files as tabs — keeps key resources one click away for everyone in the channel.
  • Click the + icon at the top of any channel
  • Add OneNote, Planner, Forms, a website, or a shared document
  • Pin your department planning document for instant access
  • Drag tabs to reorder them
11
Power
Saving & searching
Bookmark messages you need and use the powerful search bar to find anything across all your Teams conversations.
  • Hover a message → click the bookmark icon to save it
  • Saved messages: profile picture → Saved
  • Ctrl + F to search within Teams
  • Filter search results by person, date, or file type

Email management4 skills
1
Starter
Focused inbox
Outlook automatically separates important mail from low-priority items using AI — train it quickly to stay on top.
  • Your inbox splits into Focused and Other tabs
  • Right-click any email → Move to Other / Move to Focused to train it
  • Toggle off if you prefer one inbox: View → Show Focused Inbox
2
Starter
Rules & automatic sorting
Create a rule once — Outlook sorts matching emails into the right folder every time they arrive, automatically.
  • Right-click an email → Create Rule
  • Set the condition: from a specific sender, subject contains words
  • Choose an action: move to folder, mark as read, flag
  • Manage rules: Settings → View all → Mail → Rules
3
Core
Schedule send
Write the email now, deliver it at the right time — good practice for respecting colleagues' evenings and weekends.
  • Compose your email as normal
  • Click the dropdown arrow next to Send
  • Select Send later → choose date and time
  • Find it in Drafts until it sends
4
Core
Quick Parts templates
Save frequently-used text blocks and insert them in two clicks — ideal for standard responses, disclaimers, or letterheads.
  • Write and select the text you want to save
  • Insert → Quick Parts → Save Selection to Quick Part Gallery
  • To insert: email body → Insert → Quick Parts → select your block

Core
Scheduling assistant
See everyone's free and busy times before you book — no more email chains trying to agree a meeting slot.
  • Create a new meeting and add attendees in the To field
  • Click the Scheduling Assistant tab
  • Green slots show when all attendees are free — click one to fill the time

Core
Out of office reply
Set automatic replies with a date range — write separate messages for internal colleagues and external contacts.
  • File → Automatic Replies (or Settings → Automatic replies)

  • Filter inbox or calendar by category via the search bar

  • Click the flag icon on any email to add it to Tasks

  • View all flagged emails in the Tasks pane or Microsoft To Do app

Microsoft Word
Documents · Formatting · Collaboration · Accessibility
Formatting fundamentals4 skills
1
Starter
Using Styles
Heading Styles are the most important Word habit — they enable navigation panes, auto contents tables, and screen reader accessibility.
  • Use Heading 1, 2, 3 from the Styles pane — never just bold text
  • Modify a style: right-click → Modify — changes apply everywhere instantly
  • View → Navigation Pane to jump between sections
  • References → Table of Contents → Automatic for a linked contents page
2
Starter
Track changes & comments
Essential for collaborative editing, policy reviews, and giving written feedback on colleague or pupil documents.
  • Turn on: Review → Track Changes (Ctrl + Shift + E)
  • Accept or reject: Review → Accept All / Reject All to process quickly
  • Add a comment: select text → Ctrl + Alt + M
  • Resolve a comment: click the tick icon in the comment bubble
3
Core
Find & Replace (advanced)
Replace text, specific formatting, or entire fonts across a whole document instantly — far more powerful than basic search.
  • Open with Ctrl + H
  • Click More to expand options: match case, whole words, wildcards
  • Click Format to replace specific fonts or paragraph styles
4
Core
Compare documents
See exactly what changed between two versions — creates a tracked-changes view without altering either original.
  • Review → Compare → Compare
  • Select the original document and the revised version
  • Word generates a new document showing all differences as tracked changes
Power & accessibility features3 skills
5
Power
Accessibility checker
Check documents work with screen readers before sharing — especially important for pupil-facing and parent-facing resources.
  • Review → Check Accessibility
  • Word flags: missing image alt text, low contrast, missing headings
  • Click each issue for a plain English explanation and a one-click fix
6
Power
Dictation
Speak to write — useful for drafting documents quickly or for staff who find sustained keyboard typing slow or tiring.
  • Home → Dictate (microphone icon) or press Alt + `
  • Speak naturally — say "comma", "full stop", "new paragraph" for punctuation
  • Dictation works in Outlook and OneNote too
7
Power
Readability statistics
Word's Editor gives a Flesch readability score — useful when writing for parents or producing accessible pupil resources.
  • Review → Editor → Document Stats (at the bottom of the panel)
  • Flesch score above 60 = accessible for most adults
  • Long sentences and passive voice are the biggest readability drags

Microsoft Excel
Data · Formulas · Analysis · Interactive Simulator
Essential formulas for school use12 formulas
1
Starter
=SUM()
Total a range of numbers — the most used formula in Excel.
  • =SUM(B2:B30) — adds all values in B2 to B30
  • =SUM(B2,D2,F2) — adds specific cells
  • Use AutoSum: select a cell below a column and press Alt + =
  • Works across sheets: =SUM(Sheet2!B2:B30)
2
Starter
=AVERAGE()
Calculate the mean of a range — essential for class averages, tracking performance over time.
  • =AVERAGE(B2:B30) — mean of all values in range
  • Ignores empty cells automatically (unlike SUM/COUNT)
  • =AVERAGEIF(range,"condition",avg_range) — average only matching rows
  • e.g. =AVERAGEIF(A2:A30,"Year 10",C2:C30) — Year 10 average only
3
Starter
=MIN() / =MAX()
Find the lowest or highest value in a range instantly — useful for identifying outliers in pupil data.
  • =MIN(B2:B30) — returns the lowest score
  • =MAX(B2:B30) — returns the highest score
  • Combine: =MAX(B2:B30)-MIN(B2:B30) gives the range
  • Use with conditional formatting to highlight the top/bottom scorer
4
Starter
=COUNT() / =COUNTA()
Count how many cells contain numbers (COUNT) or any value at all (COUNTA) — for registers, completions, submissions.
  • =COUNT(B2:B30) — counts cells with numbers only
  • =COUNTA(B2:B30) — counts non-empty cells (text or numbers)
  • =COUNTBLANK(B2:B30) — counts empty cells (missing data)
  • Use COUNTA to check how many pupils have submitted work
5
Core
=COUNTIF() / =COUNTIFS()
Count cells meeting one or more conditions — the most useful formula for mark books and performance tracking.
  • =COUNTIF(B2:B30,"Pass") — count pupils who passed
  • =COUNTIF(B2:B30,">=70") — count pupils scoring 70 or above
  • =COUNTIFS(A2:A30,"Yr10",B2:B30,">=70") — multiple conditions
  • Use <> for "not equal to": =COUNTIF(B2:B30,"<>Absent")
6
Core
=SUMIF() / =SUMIFS()
Total values that meet a condition — e.g. total marks for a specific year group, class, or teacher.
  • =SUMIF(A2:A30,"Year 10",C2:C30) — sum C where A = "Year 10"
  • =SUMIFS(C2:C30,A2:A30,"Year 10",B2:B30,"Science") — two conditions
  • Condition column and sum column can be different ranges
  • Wildcards work: =SUMIF(A2:A30,"Yr*",C2:C30) — matches Yr10, Yr11 etc.
7
Core
=XLOOKUP()
Find a value in one column and return a corresponding value from another — the modern replacement for VLOOKUP.
  • =XLOOKUP(A2, Register!A:A, Register!B:B)
  • Looks up A2's value in the Register sheet, returns column B
  • Add a 4th argument: =XLOOKUP(A2,A:A,B:B,"Not found")
  • Works left-to-right AND right-to-left — VLOOKUP cannot go left
8
Core
=VLOOKUP() (legacy)
The classic lookup — still widely used and worth knowing. Looks right from a key column. Use XLOOKUP in new sheets.
  • =VLOOKUP(lookup_value, table_array, col_index, FALSE)
  • =VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!A:C,3,FALSE) — finds A2 in Sheet2 col A, returns col 3
  • Always use FALSE as the 4th argument for exact match
  • Cannot look left — the key column must be the leftmost in the range
9
Core
=IF() and nested IF
Return different values depending on whether a condition is true — essential for grading, flags, and status labels.
  • =IF(B2>=70,"Pass","Fail") — simple pass/fail
  • =IF(B2>=70,"Merit",IF(B2>=50,"Pass","Fail")) — nested for grades
  • For multiple tiers: use IFS() for cleaner syntax
  • =IFS(B2>=70,"A",B2>=60,"B",B2>=50,"C",TRUE,"Below")
10
Core
=IFERROR()
Wrap any formula to suppress error messages — keeps spreadsheets clean when data is incomplete.
  • =IFERROR(XLOOKUP(A2,B:B,C:C),"Not found")
  • Replaces #N/A, #VALUE!, #REF! errors with your chosen text
  • Use "" to show a blank instead of an error
  • Essential when sharing spreadsheets with staff who find errors alarming
11
Power
=RANK() / =PERCENTRANK()
Rank a pupil's score within a class — instantly see where each pupil sits relative to their peers.
  • =RANK(B2,B$2:B$30,0) — rank B2 within range, descending (0=highest=1st)
  • Use $ to lock the range when copying the formula down
  • =RANK(B2,B$2:B$30,1) — ascending rank (lowest = 1)
  • =PERCENTRANK(B$2:B$30,B2)*100 — percentile position (0–100)
12
Power
=STDEV() / =MEDIAN()
Go beyond averages — standard deviation shows spread of scores; median shows the middle value, unaffected by outliers.
  • =STDEV(B2:B30) — standard deviation of scores (how spread out they are)
  • =MEDIAN(B2:B30) — the middle value when sorted
  • A large STDEV means wide variation — useful for spotting inconsistency
  • Compare AVERAGE vs MEDIAN: a big difference signals outliers skewing the data

Starter
Slide Master
Edit the Slide Master once to apply consistent fonts, colours, and branding across every slide in the deck.

Morph transition
Smooth animated movement between slides with zero manual animation work — duplicate a slide, move something, apply Morph.

  • On the duplicate: Transitions → Morph
  • PowerPoint animates all differences automatically

Designer & alt text
Designer suggests polished layouts; alt text on images is a legal accessibility requirement — do both together.
  • Add content → Designer panel opens right (or Design → Designer)

Core
Rehearse Timings
Practice your delivery with a per-slide timer — see exactly where you over-run before the real presentation.

OneDrive
Cloud storage · File sharing · Version history · Sync
Essential knowledge4 skills
1
Starter
OneDrive vs SharePoint
Knowing where to save which file is the most important OneDrive skill — saving in the wrong place makes sharing harder.
  • OneDrive = your personal cloud. For files only you need
  • SharePoint = shared drives for teams, departments, whole school
  • Files shared via Teams are stored in SharePoint automatically
  • Rule: if a colleague might ever need it, save it to SharePoint
2
Starter
Version history
Every edit to a OneDrive or SharePoint file is preserved — restore any previous version at any time.
  • Right-click any file in OneDrive → Version history
  • In Word / Excel / PowerPoint: File → Info → Version History
  • Click any version to preview it
  • Click Restore to make that version the current file
3
Core
Sharing files safely
Shared links can be set to view-only, edit-enabled, or restricted to specific people — always check before sharing externally.
  • Right-click any file → Share
  • Click link settings: Anyone / People in school / Specific people
  • Toggle Allow editing on or off as appropriate
  • Set an expiry date for links shared outside school
4
Core
Sync to your desktop
Sync OneDrive folders to your PC — files appear in File Explorer and work offline, syncing when reconnected.
  • Open the OneDrive app (cloud icon in system tray)
  • Sign in with your school Microsoft account
  • Choose which folders to sync to your machine
  • Right-click a synced file → Always keep on this device for offline access
Power features2 skills
5
Power
Real-time co-authoring
Multiple people can edit the same Word, Excel, or PowerPoint file simultaneously — each person's cursor appears in a different colour.
  • Save your file to OneDrive or SharePoint (not your local C: drive)
  • Share the file with colleagues
  • Everyone's changes appear live — no more emailing different versions
6
Power
Recycle bin recovery
Deleted files stay in the OneDrive Recycle Bin for 93 days — you can restore them at any point within that window.
  • Go to onedrive.com → sign in with your school account
  • Click Recycle bin in the left sidebar
  • Select the file → Restore to return it to its original location
  • After 93 days, permanently deleted files cannot be recovered

Enterprise data protection — what this means in practice

When using Copilot through your school Microsoft 365 account, your prompts and data are protected by Microsoft's enterprise commitments: not used to train AI models, not shared with third parties, processed within Microsoft's secure cloud. This applies to Microsoft 365 Copilot accessed via your school login. It does not apply to the free public Copilot at copilot.microsoft.com when using a personal account.

GDPR reminder — applies regardless of enterprise protection

Never enter real pupil names, identifiable data, SEND details, or safeguarding information into Copilot or any AI tool. Use [Pupil] as a placeholder. Enterprise protection covers your organisational data — it does not change your obligations under UK GDPR or Keeping Children Safe in Education.

Microsoft Excel — Formula Reference
School mark book & data management · UK school contexts
Totals, Averages & Counting
=SUM(B2:B30) — total a range. Use Alt + = for AutoSum shortcut.
=AVERAGE(B2:B30) — arithmetic mean; ignores blank cells automatically.
=AVERAGEIF(A:A,"Yr10",C:C) — average only rows matching a condition (e.g. one year group).
=MEDIAN(B2:B30) — middle value; not skewed by outliers. Compare with AVERAGE to spot skew.
=MIN(B2:B30) / =MAX(B2:B30) — lowest and highest score in a range. Subtract to get the spread.
=COUNT(B2:B30) — count numeric cells. COUNTA includes text. COUNTBLANK counts empty cells.
=STDEV(B2:B30) — standard deviation; shows how spread out the results are across a cohort.
=RANK(B2,B$2:B$30,0) — rank a pupil's score in the class (0 = highest first). Lock range with $ when copying down.
Conditional Formulas
=COUNTIF(B2:B30,"Pass") — count cells matching one condition. Use >=, <>, and wildcards (* ?).
=COUNTIFS(A:A,"Yr10",B:B,">=70") — count with two or more conditions simultaneously.
=SUMIF(A:A,"Yr10",C:C) — total column C only where column A equals the condition.
=SUMIFS(C:C,A:A,"Yr10",B:B,"Science") — sum with two or more conditions.
Logic & Grade Banding
=IF(B2>=70,"Pass","Fail") — returns one of two values depending on whether the condition is true.
=IFS(B2>=80,"A",B2>=65,"B",B2>=50,"C",TRUE,"U") — cleaner multi-tier grade banding. No nesting.
=IFERROR(formula,"Not found") — replaces #N/A, #VALUE!, #REF! errors with your chosen text.
Lookups — Pulling Data Between Sheets
=XLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!A:A,Sheet2!B:B,"Not found") — modern lookup. Works left and right; 4th argument handles missing values gracefully.
=VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!A:C,3,FALSE) — legacy lookup. Key column must be leftmost. Always use FALSE for exact match.
Essential Habits
Ctrl + T — Format as Table before adding formulas. New rows inherit formulas and formatting automatically.
$ in references — write B$2:B$30 (not B2:B30) to lock the range when copying a formula down a column.
Ctrl + E — Flash Fill. Type one example, press Ctrl + E, and Excel fills the pattern for the whole column.
PivotTable — Insert → PivotTable. Summarise hundreds of rows in under a minute; no formulas needed.

Microsoft Teams Essentials
Channels — organise team conversations by topic. Use tabs (Files, Notes) within channels to centralise resources.
Scheduling a meeting — Calendar → New meeting. A joining link is added automatically to every attendee's calendar invite.
Files tab — files in Teams are stored in SharePoint. Version-controlled, simultaneously editable, accessible to the whole team.
Recording meetings — three dots → Start recording. Saves to OneDrive automatically; appears in meeting chat once processed.
Transcription — More actions → Start transcription. Required for Copilot meeting summaries. Download as Word document.
@mentions — @name notifies one person, @channel notifies all channel members, @team notifies everyone. Use sparingly.
Outlook Essentials
Focused Inbox — View → Focused Inbox. Automatically separates important emails from newsletters and notifications.
Rules — Home → Rules → Create Rule. Auto-file, flag, or forward emails matching your conditions.
BCC for group emails — always BCC parents in group messages, never CC. CC exposes all email addresses to every recipient — a GDPR risk.
Delay delivery — Options → Delay Delivery. Schedule an email to send at a set time — useful for out-of-hours drafting.
Recall message — Sent Items → open email → Message → Actions → Recall. Only works if the recipient hasn't opened it yet.
Quick Steps — Home → Quick Steps → New. Create one-click actions: e.g. file + mark as read + flag simultaneously.
